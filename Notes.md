### Geometric Series Reference: 
$$ 1 + r + \dots + r^n = \frac{1 - r^{n+1}}{1 - r} $$ if $|r| < 1$ and $n \to \infty$
$$ 1 + r + \dots = \frac{1}{1 - r} $$

### These are Euler’s identities used often in Signals & Systems and Fourier analysis.
```horizontal
$$
e^{j\theta} = \cos \theta + j \sin \theta  
$$

$$
e^{-j\theta} = \cos \theta - j \sin \theta  
$$

$$
e^{j\theta} + e^{-j\theta} = 2\cos\theta
$$

$$
\cos\theta = \frac{e^{j\theta} + e^{-j\theta}}{2}
$$
---
$$
e^{j\theta} + e^{-j\theta} = 2\cos \theta  
$$
 
$$
e^{j\theta} - e^{-j\theta} = 2j\sin \theta  
$$

$$
e^{j\theta} - e^{-j\theta} = 2j\sin\theta
$$
$$
\sin\theta = \frac{e^{j\theta} - e^{-j\theta}}{2j}
$$
```
